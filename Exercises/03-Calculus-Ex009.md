# Exercise 009: Integration by Parts — The Product Rule in Reverse

## The Formula

Integration by parts is the reverse of the product rule:

\[
\int u \, dv = uv - \int v \, du
\]

The art is in choosing which part of your integrand to set as **u** (to differentiate) and which as **dv** (to integrate).

## Problem 1 — Classic Starter

\[
\int x \, e^x \, dx
\]

*Hint: Which part gets simpler when you differentiate it?*

## Problem 2 — The Logarithm

\[
\int \ln x \, dx
\]

*Hint: There's only one function here. Let dv = dx.*

## Problem 3 — The Cyclic One

\[
\int e^x \sin x \, dx
\]

*Hint: Apply by parts twice. You'll get the original integral back — solve for it algebraically.*

## Problem 4 — Reduction Formula

\[
\int x^2 e^x \, dx
\]

*Hint: Let u = x², dv = e^x dx. You'll get an integral you just solved in Problem 1.*
