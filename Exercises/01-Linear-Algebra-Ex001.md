# Exercise: Vectors, Span, and Basis

## Problem

You are studying a quantum two-level system (like a spin-1/2 particle). The system has a natural basis $\{\,|0\rangle,\,|1\rangle\,\}$, which you can think of as vectors:
$$
|0\rangle = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad |1\rangle = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}.
$$

A colleague uses a *rotated* basis:
$$
|+\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}, \quad |-\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}.
$$

### Part A
Is $\{|+\rangle, |-\rangle\}$ a valid basis for the same quantum space? Why or why not? (Consider span and linear independence.)

### Part B
Express the vector $|\psi\rangle = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}$ in the rotated basis. That is, find $a,b$ such that
$$
|\psi\rangle = a|+\rangle + b|-\rangle.
$$

### Part C
Does the physical state $|\psi\rangle$ change when you switch from the standard basis to the rotated basis, or only its numerical representation? Explain in one sentence.
