# Quick Review Quiz — Complex Numbers (Ex002)

Answer without calculators.

## Symbol guide

- \(i\): imaginary unit, \(i^2=-1\)
- \(|z|\) or \(r\): modulus (magnitude) of \(z\)
- \(\theta\): argument (angle) of \(z\)
- \(\overline{z}\): complex conjugate of \(z\)
- \(e^{i\theta}\): Euler form, equals \(\cos\theta+i\sin\theta\)

## Q1

Write in polar (exponential) form with principal argument:

$$
z=1-\sqrt{3}\,i
$$

## Q2

Compute:

$$
(1+i)^8
$$

## Q3

Solve for all roots:

$$
z^4=16
$$

Give answers in exponential form.

## Q4

If \(z=a+bi\), express \(|z|^2\) in terms of \(z\) and \(\overline{z}\):

$$
|z|^2 = ?
$$

State one reason this identity is useful in physics/math.

## Learner responses

1. Polar form: \(z=2e^{-i\pi/3}\)
2. \((1+i)^8=16\)
3. Fourth roots: \(2,\quad 2e^{i\pi/2},\quad 2e^{i\pi},\quad 2e^{i3\pi/2}\)
4. Identity: \(|z|^2=z\cdot\overline{z}\). Reason: relates the magnitude of a complex number to its product with conjugate (inner product structure).
