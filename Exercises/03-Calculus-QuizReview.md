# Calculus Review Quiz: Limits → Integration by Parts

## Problem 1 — Limits

Evaluate:

\[
\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin(3x)}{x}
\]

(Hint: recall \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1\))

---

## Problem 2 — Differentiation

Find the derivative:

\[
\frac{d}{dx} \left[ \ln\left( x^2 + 1 \right) \right]
\]

## Problem 3 — Chain Rule

## Bonus — Quantum Connection

Normalisation constants often involve Gaussian integrals. Evaluate:

\[
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x^2 e^{-x^2} \, dx
\]

You may use the known result \(\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} dx = \sqrt{\pi}\). No integration by parts required here if you think about symmetry and differentiation under the integral sign... but it can also be done with parts.
