# Exercise 7: Finding Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

## Problem

Find all eigenvalues and eigenvectors of:

$$A = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$$

**Steps to follow:**
1. Write the characteristic equation $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$
2. Solve for $\lambda$
3. For each $\lambda$, find the corresponding eigenvector

## Quantum Connection

Once you find the eigenvectors, ask yourself: if $A$ were a quantum observable (like an energy operator), what would the eigenvalues represent physically?
