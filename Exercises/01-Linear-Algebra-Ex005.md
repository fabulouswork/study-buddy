# Exercise 5: Matrix Inverse (Undoing a Transformation)

## Problem

Consider the matrix:

$$M = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$$

**Find the inverse matrix $M^{-1}$** such that $M \cdot M^{-1} = I$ (the identity matrix).

*Note: You can use the formula for 2×2 inverse if you know it, or derive it from first principles.*

## Interpretation

Once you've found $M^{-1}$, explain: what does it mean (geometrically) to multiply a vector by $M^{-1}$ after multiplying it by $M$?

---

**Hint (if needed):** What do you expect $M \cdot M^{-1}$ to equal? Why?
