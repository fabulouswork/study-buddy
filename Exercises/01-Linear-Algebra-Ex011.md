# Quick Review Quiz — Linear Algebra (Ex011)

Answer without calculators.

## Symbol guide

- \(\lambda\) (lambda): eigenvalue symbol
- \(\langle a,b\rangle\): inner product (dot product in real vectors)
- \(\|v\|\): norm (vector length)

## Q1

Let

$$
A=
\begin{pmatrix}
2 & 1 \\
1 & 2
\end{pmatrix}
$$

Find the eigenvalues \(\lambda\) of \(A\).

## Q2

Are the vectors

$$
v_1=(1,1,0),\quad v_2=(1,0,1),\quad v_3=(0,1,1)
$$

linearly independent? Briefly justify.

## Q3

Compute the dot product and angle type (acute/right/obtuse):

$$
u=(2,-1),\quad w=(1,3)
$$

## Q4

If \(\|\psi\|=1\) and \(\phi\) is orthogonal to \(\psi\), what is

$$
\langle \psi,\phi\rangle
$$

and why is this important in quantum-state interpretation?

## Learner responses

1. Characteristic equation attempt: \((2-\lambda)^2-1=0\)
1. Eigenvalues: \(\lambda=1,3\)
2. Reasoning: Assume \(v_1 = a v_2 + b v_3\). Component comparison gives \(a=1\), \(b=1\), and \(a+b=0\), i.e. \(2=0\), contradiction. Concluded the vectors are linearly independent.
3. Dot product: \(u\cdot w=-1\)
3. Angle type: obtuse
4. Inner product: \(\langle\psi,\phi\rangle=0\). Note: orthogonal states can be used as basis states.
