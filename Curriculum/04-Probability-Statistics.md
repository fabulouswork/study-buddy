# Probability & Statistics

## Relevance to Quantum Physics

Quantum mechanics is inherently probabilistic. Measurement outcomes are random variables; the Born rule gives probabilities from amplitudes. Expectation values of observables map directly to statistical expectation. Understanding probability deeply is essential for interpreting quantum results.

## Subtopics

1. **Probability Fundamentals** — sample spaces, events, axioms
2. **Random Variables & Distributions** — discrete and continuous, PDFs, CDFs
3. **Expectation & Variance** — mean, spread, standard deviation → uncertainty principle
4. **Conditional Probability & Bayes' Theorem** — updating beliefs
5. **Quantum Probability Interpretation** — Born rule, $|\psi|^2$, measurement as sampling
