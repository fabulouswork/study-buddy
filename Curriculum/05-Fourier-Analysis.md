# Fourier Analysis

## Relevance to Quantum Physics

The Fourier transform connects position-space and momentum-space wavefunctions. The Heisenberg uncertainty principle is a direct consequence of the mathematics of Fourier pairs. Solving the Schrödinger equation often involves decomposing wavefunctions into frequency components.

## Subtopics

1. **Periodic Functions & Harmonics** — sine, cosine, superposition
2. **Fourier Series** — decomposing periodic functions into harmonics
3. **Fourier Transform** — extending to non-periodic functions, frequency domain
4. **Convolution Theorem** — multiplication in one domain = convolution in the other
5. **Uncertainty Principle via Fourier** — bandwidth-duration trade-off → $\Delta x \, \Delta p \geq \hbar/2$
